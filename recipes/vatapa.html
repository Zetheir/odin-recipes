Vatapá Recipe

Description

Vatapá is a typical afro-brazilian dish. It comes from the ioruba term "vata'pa". It is a symbol of the black skinned population that lives in the region. It's widely known in Bahia, where in it's making they add dendê oil, and is served as a side dish to acarajé. Try out this delicious bahian dish!

  • 1 teacup of brazil nuts
  • 1 teacup of toasted peanuts
  • 500ml of coconut milk
  • 2 teacups of headless dry shrimp
  • 1 big onion
  • 2 sprigs of coriander
  • 1/2 teacup of flour
  • A dash of dendê oil
  1. Cut big pieces of onions. No need to have it small, they'll be cut in the blender.
  2. In the blener, add coconut milk, brazil nuts, toasted peanuts, onions and full sprigs of coriander. Blend them until it becomes a homogenous paste.
  3. Put the shrimps and blend until the blender starts to look a bit weak.
  4. If the paste looks to liquid, add a bit of flour. If it looks to thick, add water. At this point, the paste should already taste exactly like vatapá.
  5. Put the paste into a pan and add the dendê oil. Mix throughly in high heat until it gets it's characteristic color.
  6. Serve.
